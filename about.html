Meet the founder

Gabriel Mills

Founder & Creative Director, GM Creatives

Where it started

GM Creatives grew out of over 5 years in the creative industry spent moving between graphic design, web development, and app development rather than staying in just one lane. That path wasn't an accident. Every discipline added to the mix made the others stronger. Design work builds sharper technical judgment. Development work grounds design decisions in real technical constraints instead of ignoring them.

Most creative studios split those skills across different people who rarely talk to each other directly. GM Creatives closes that gap with one team that understands the full picture, from the first sketch of a logo to the last line of code that ships a website.

Why GM Creatives exists

GM Creatives was built on a simple idea: brands shouldn't need five different freelancers (a designer, a developer, a social media specialist) to hold together. One studio, one point of contact, and a team personally invested in getting every single project right.

That means no account managers relaying messages, no work quietly handed off to a subcontractor you've never spoken to, and no project moving forward until you're actually happy with the direction. If something's not working, you're speaking directly with the people fixing it.

How we work

Design and engineering are treated as one process, not two handoffs. A brand identity is built with the website it'll live on in mind. A website is built with the performance and maintainability of real code in mind, not just how it looks in a static mockup. That's the advantage of a team fluent in both design and development: nothing gets lost in translation between the design and the build.

What GM Creatives brings to a project

Design + code fluency

Years of experience across both sides means visual decisions and technical ones get made together, not in isolation.

Direct communication

You talk directly with the people doing the work, every time, with no relayed messages and no lost context.

End-to-end ownership

From first concept to post-launch support, one team is accountable for the outcome.

No template shortcuts

Every brand and every build starts from your goals, not a drag-and-drop starting point.
